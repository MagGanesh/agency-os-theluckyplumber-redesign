# Redesign Brief — The Lucky Plumber

## Service / Industry
Residential and commercial plumbing and gas boiler services, South East London, UK. Services include emergency callouts, central heating installation, boiler installation/servicing/repair, drain cleaning, and general plumbing repairs. Covers Dulwich, Croydon, Lambeth, Southwark, Lewisham, Greenwich, Westminster, Wandsworth, Kensington & Chelsea, Mitcham & Morden, and Sutton.

---

## Target Audience & Tone
**Primary audience:** Homeowners and renters in South East London facing urgent or planned plumbing/heating needs — people searching for a reliable, locally-known tradesperson they can call today.

**Secondary audience:** Landlords and small businesses needing gas safety certificates, boiler servicing, or maintenance contracts.

**Tone:** Trustworthy, straight-talking, and local. Not corporate. Warm but professional — the tone of an experienced tradesperson who shows up on time, tells you the truth, and gets the job done. Confident claims backed by specific proof (years of experience, named boiler brands, real service areas). Urgency is appropriate given the 24/7 emergency positioning.

## Color Palette

The logo uses a warm gold (#C49A1A approx.) and near-black charcoal — the "lucky" horseshoe-and-wrench mark is unmistakably warm and artisan. The current site over-applies the gold as flat backgrounds everywhere, draining it of impact. The redesign should use the gold sparingly as a premium accent, pairing it with a deep navy that conveys trust and authority — a combination that reads as both reliable and modern in the UK trades market.

**Primary:** #1B3A5C — Deep Navy
Conveys trust, authority, and reliability. Dominant colour for header, hero, and key CTA elements.

**Accent:** #C49A1A — Warm Gold
Pulled directly from the logo. Used for highlights, CTAs, headings, icon fills, and hover states. Restores its impact by using it selectively.

**Background:** #FFFFFF — White
Clean page backgrounds for content sections.

**Surface:** #F4F3F0 — Warm Off-White
Subtle warm grey for card backgrounds, alternating sections, and form fields. Prevents the page feeling sterile.

**Dark Surface:** #0F2640 — Midnight Navy
For footer background and hero overlays — slightly darker than Primary for depth.

**Text:** #1C1C1E — Near Black
High-contrast body copy throughout.

**Text Muted:** #6B7280 — Cool Grey
Subheadings, captions, form placeholders, metadata.

---

## Typography Pairing

**Heading: Montserrat (Bold / ExtraBold)**
A geometric sans-serif with strong authority and excellent legibility at large sizes. Its confident, uppercase-friendly letterforms suit a trades business that needs to project competence without feeling stiff. Works well reversed out of navy.

**Body: Inter (Regular / Medium)**
The gold standard for readable UI copy at small sizes. Neutral and professional without being cold. Pairs naturally with Montserrat — both geometric, both highly legible on screen, both available via Google Fonts.

## Design Direction Notes

- **Clean and modern trades aesthetic:** White and navy foundation with gold accents. Avoids the cluttered, template-heavy look of the current site. Think Pimlico Plumbers or British Gas's marketing — confident, local authority.
- **Mobile-first hierarchy:** Hero headline and phone CTA must be the first two things visible above the fold on mobile. The contact form moves to a dedicated section or sticky bottom bar on mobile — never stacked in the hero.
- **Gold used sparingly for maximum impact:** Gold appears on CTAs, icon fills, stat numbers, and key heading highlights only. Never as a solid section background.
- **Personality over stock copy:** The horseshoe logo gives the brand genuine character. A section with a photo of the plumber, a personal name, and a plain-English guarantee ("I show up on time, I quote fairly, I clean up after") would be a high-conversion differentiator from competitors.
- **Trust signals front and centre:** Gas Safe badge, boiler brand logos, experience stats (corrected and accurate), and real testimonials must appear on every key page — not buried below the fold.
