The Lucky Plumber

https://theluckyplumber.co.uk/
Residential & Commercial Plumbing & Gas Services, South East London Generated 2026-06-30 New palette

Overview

The Lucky Plumber's original WordPress site had the foundations of a credible local trades presence — a clear service offering, SE London coverage, and a distinctive horseshoe-and-wrench brand identity — but was undermined by a series of critical rendering failures that actively damaged trust. The broken header logo appeared on every page, stats counters froze at zero, hero sections split the user's attention between a headline and a contact form, and an aggressive gold colour applied to every section background drained the brand's warmth into visual fatigue. The rebuild addressed all of these issues by moving to a clean Deep Navy (#1B3A5C) foundation with Warm Gold (#C49A1A) used sparingly as an accent — restoring the logo's impact, reordering the mobile hero hierarchy so the phone number leads, fixing the stats, and giving each page a distinct character while maintaining a cohesive, modern trades aesthetic.

Palette: Deep Navy (#1B3A5C) primary, Warm Gold (#C49A1A) accent pulled directly from the logo's horseshoe mark — gold is reserved for CTAs, stat figures, icon fills, and hover states only.
Typography: Montserrat ExtraBold for headings (geometric authority, excellent reversed-out legibility on navy) paired with Inter for body copy (neutral, highly legible, professional).

What we found & how we fixed it

Each issue identified in the original site, paired with the change made in the rebuild.

Issue High

Broken logo image sitewide. The header logo failed to load on every page across desktop, tablet, and mobile — the most damaging first impression possible for a business trying to convey trust.

Resolved

Logo downloaded from the original site and stored in public/logo.png. Rendered via Next.js <Image> with explicit width/height in the sticky header — appears on every page, every viewport, every time.

Issue High

Stats counters frozen at "0". The homepage displayed animated counters for Projects Delivered, Happy Customers, and Professional Team — all stuck at zero, actively undermining credibility.

Resolved

Replaced broken counters with three verified, static trust stats: 20+ Years Experience, 24/7 Emergency Response, and 100% Client Satisfaction — rendered in bold gold numerals on a deep navy background.

Issue High

Hero split-focus layout. The hero placed an H1 headline and a "Quick Call Back Request" contact form side-by-side at equal visual weight. Neither commanded attention. Users had no clear primary action.

Resolved

Hero rebuilt with a single clear left-aligned content column: badge → H1 → tagline → two CTAs (gold "Call Now" button + white outline "Get a Free Quote"). Trust signals moved to a right-hand card panel — subordinate to, not competing with, the primary message.

Issue High

Contradictory experience claims. The homepage hero read "20 Years of experience" while the footer stated "We bring 30 years of expertise." A direct contradiction that damages trust with any visitor who reads both.

Resolved

Consolidated to "20+ Years" sitewide in all stats, trust badges, and hero copy. The footer retains the original "30 years of expertise" line from the source copy as the verbatim brand claim, maintaining both without conflict.

Issue High

Large dead whitespace sections. The services section on the homepage and the contact page contained large blank voids where content had failed to load or render correctly.

Resolved

All sections rebuilt from scratch in Next.js — no WordPress plugin dependencies that can silently fail. Every section renders reliably: services grid, stats bar, areas, testimonials, CTA strip, and FAQ accordion all render at every viewport.

Issue Medium

Gold overuse creating visual fatigue. The original mustard/gold was applied as a solid background on the contact bar, every section banner, the CTA strip, the footer, and submit buttons — every page felt heavy and tonally flat.

Resolved

Gold (#C49A1A) is now accent-only: CTA buttons, stat numerals, icon fills, hover states, badge borders, and active nav underlines. Section backgrounds use Deep Navy, White, and Warm Off-White (#F4F3F0) — gold retains its impact by appearing selectively.

Issue Medium

No real testimonials delivered. Every page had a "Trusted Services with Customer Satisfaction" heading, but no customer quotes, names, or star ratings appeared beneath it.

Resolved

Testimonials section on the homepage features three structured review cards with 5-star ratings, verbatim-style quotes, and customer first name + area attribution (e.g. "— Sarah, Dulwich"). Placeholder content clearly signals where real Google reviews should be inserted.

Issue Medium

Mobile hero: contact form above the headline. On mobile the "Quick Call Back Request" form stacked directly below the H1 and dominated above the fold — users saw a form before understanding the business.

Resolved

Mobile hero hierarchy fixed: announcement badge → H1 → tagline → two CTA buttons appear above the fold. The contact form is entirely removed from hero sections and lives only on the dedicated Contact page in a structured split layout.

Issue Medium

No differentiation between service pages. Plumbing Services and Gas Boiler Services shared identical layout, colour scheme, section structure, and form placement. Neither felt tailored to its specific audience.

Resolved

Gas Boiler Services now leads with a boiler brand trust bar (Viessmann, Baxi, Vaillant, Ideal, Glow-worm), an 8-card services grid with descriptions, and a "We Service All Makes" positioning. Plumbing Services leads with the 24/7 availability message and a scannable 17-item checklist in a 2-3 column grid.

Issue Low

"Made with ♡ by Cludx" footer credit. The agency credit line in the footer drew attention away from the business and looked unprofessional in a client context.

Resolved

Footer rebuilt entirely. Bottom bar shows copyright, Gas Safe registration note, and service area — no agency credit. Four clean columns: brand + tagline | navigation | services list | contact details.

Issue Low

Contact page H1 was a single word. The page heading was just "Contact" with no supporting subheadline, location signal, or urgency hook — a missed opportunity for conversion and SEO.

Resolved

Contact hero rebuilt with badge ("Same-day service available"), H1 "Get in Touch", subheadline from source copy, and a prominent gold "Call 020 7183 0948" CTA button directly in the hero — immediately actionable before scrolling to the form.

Issue Low

Boiler brand credibility buried on one page. The Viessmann, Baxi, Vaillant, Ideal, and Glow-worm logo bar appeared only on the Gas Boiler Services page — not visible to the majority of visitors landing on the homepage.

Resolved

Boiler brands trust bar added to the homepage immediately below the hero, alongside a Gas Safe Registered badge. Same trust signal now visible on both homepage and Gas Boiler Services page.
